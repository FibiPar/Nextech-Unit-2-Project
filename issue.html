Why is this an issue?

IDRA surveys assert that “nearly two-thirds of students specifically request lessons on race, history, current events, and forms of discrimination that they have experienced… Restricting conversations that matter to students’ safety—like racism— makes schools less safe for students seeking guidance, and basic information.”


The University of the People, an online degree college program, states that “understanding past events and how they impact the world today can bring about empathy.” “Empathy is the person’s ability to comprehend the other’s position, even though he does not have the direct experience to do so,” according to Elisabeth D. Lazarakou, a Ph.D. researcher at the National University of Athens.
